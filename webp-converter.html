Frequently Asked Questions

What makes WebP superior to JPG and PNG?

WebP was constructed by Google to merge the best of both world formatting layers: highly sophisticated lossy compression algorithms (like JPEG) paired with alpha-channel transparency structures (like PNG). WebP consistently delivers smaller files with virtually zero visual fidelity loss.

Are WebP images supported across all browsers?

Yes! As of 2026, over 97% of worldwide active web clients completely support native rendering loads of WebP, including Google Chrome, Apple Safari, Mozilla Firefox, and Microsoft Edge. Reverting WebPs back to JPG is only necessary for legacy applications or device setups.
