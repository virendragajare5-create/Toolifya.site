Frequently Asked Questions

What happens to transparent alpha-layers when converting to JPEG?

JPEG structures cannot physically accommodate transparency overlays as they map color channels on strict 24-bit matrices. If color maps are converted, transparent sections of PNGs are backfilled using your custom selected hex backdrop color picker (defaulting to white).

Does converting PNG to JPEG reduce storage size?

Generally yes! PNGs utilize compression algorithms called deflate to keep graphics mathematically lossless, requiring more storage. JPEGs discard excess color depth rendering, saving up to 80% on file storage sizes.
